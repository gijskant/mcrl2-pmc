\noindent
System identifiers are identifiers that are generated by the system, in both
declarations and expressions. System identifiers are different from user
identifiers in that they have a special use. This document describes all system
identifiers and their use.

In the following sections, we treat system identifiers for sorts, operations,
variables, actions and processes, respectively. In each section, a table is
provided which lists the name, description and external representation of each
identifier.  Because system identifiers can be generated from other system or
user identifiers, we frequently use variables to represent these identifiers.
In the external representation columns, occurring variables denote the external
representations of the substitutable terms.

\section{Sorts}

For sorts, we have the following system identifiers:

\begin{table}[!htb]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{l|R|l|L}
Name & \text{Arity} & Description & \text{External repr.}\\
\hline
Bool   & 0      & booleans         & \srtbool\\
Pos    & 0      & positive numbers & \srtnat\\
Nat    & 0      & natural numbers  & \srtnat\\
Int    & 0      & integers         & \srtnat\\
List   & 1      & lists            & \fa{List}{\_}\\
Set    & 1      & sets             & \fa{Set}{\_}\\
Bag    & 1      & bags             & \fa{Bag}{\_}\\
Struct & 1      & structured sort  & \_ = \textbf{struct} \ldots\\
Arrow  & \geq 2 & function sort    & \_ \times \cdots \times \_ \To \_\\
\end{tabular}
\caption{System identifiers for sorts}
\label{tab:sid:sorts}
\end{table}
